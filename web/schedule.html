Topic Proposal for Major Paper 2 due: Send me by e-mail (to ebb8 at pitt.edu) your topic proposals for the research paper. This is worth Response Paper points to you, and will help you and me to make sure you have a good start on the assignment. Here's how to write your topic proposal:

  • Please do this in the body of an e-mail message (not by attachment), to help me read and respond quickly! Be sure that your name is in the e-mail message and you have used a clear subject line (Topic Proposal for 19th-c Brit Lit Paper).
  • Explain what text you're going to research, which option you're choosing, and specifically what you're investigating in the text you're discussing (so you're not just using my language on the assignment sheet, but explaining how you're responding to the assignment option).
  • Locate and identify at least two good sources from the Pitt Digital Library that you're thinking of using in your paper. List these as you would on a Works Cited page so I can find them. Don't worry about formatting indents precisely; concentrate on making sure that you have all parts you need for the citation. Please do NOT include links into the databases because they do not work and just create a mess. Instead, simply indicate the name of the library database (e.g. Project Muse or JSTOR, or 19th-century UK Periodicals) in which you found the article.
  • If you're on the fence about a topic, explain what you're thinking of investigating, describe multiple topics, and try locating sources for them.

I'll respond (usually pretty quickly) with an e-mail message (so keep checking your Pitt e-mail or check with me when I see you in class/on campus). I'll either approve your topic or ask you to return to the "drawing board"--with some advice and questions to help. I may also send you to some good sources to consult, so this should be a useful process for you to get a strong and prompt start on your paper.
